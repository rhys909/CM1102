Napster's Legal Troubles

The nature of any Peer-to-Peer sharing service leaves it open to misuse. In the case of Napster, it's users began to share copyrighted material. In particular this lead Napster to recieve alot of backlash most famously from the metal group Metallica, This was due to Metallica finding their demo song "I Disappear".

In 2000, due to argued copyright infringement under the US Digital Millennium Copyright Act A&M Records and a number of other record companies sued Napster.

Napster faced the following allegations from the music industry:

  1. That Napster's Users were directly violaiting the record industries copyrights.
  2. Napster was also responsible for contributory infringement of the copyrights.
  3. Napster were also responsible for any copyright infringement done by another person.

Following the legal battle, In 2002 Napster officially announced it's bankruptcy and sold their assets. However whilst they recieved alot of backlash they also recieved alot of public support famously from Chuck D and Public Enemy but also from alot of independant artists who put alot of their success down to the fanbase they acquired through Napster.
