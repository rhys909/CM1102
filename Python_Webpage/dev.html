Guido van Rossum

Guido van Rossum is a Dutch programmer born on 31st January 1956, in Haarlem, Netherlands. He was the mind behind the Python programming language.

In 1982, Guido van Rossum achieved a masters degree in Mathematics and Comp Sci from the Uni of Amsterdam. Using the Python programming he created Grail which was a web browser coded in Python, He also was a part of HTML standard discussions.

In his life he has worked for a number of research institutes across the world, such as the Centrum Wiskunde & Informatica and the Corporation for National Research Initiatives.

In the December of 1989 Guido began the development of the Python Interpreter, he decided to name it after the Monty Python films. He took inspiration from ABC and SETL. He continued to work on and improve Python as the Benevolent dictator for life (BDFL), having done this for around 30 years he stepped down from this role in July of this year.

In his life he has recieved a number of awards:-

  • 2001, Award for the Advancement of Free Software
  • 2003, NLUUG Award
  • 2006, became a recognized engineer by the Association for Computing Machinery
  • 2018, became a Fellow of the Computer History Museum

Python Software Foundation

Formed on March 6th 2001 The Python Software Foundation is an organization whose main goal is the betterment of open source software, that uses or is related to the Python programming language. They are also a non profit organization which is tax exempt due to the US Internal Revenue Code 501(c)(3), This deems it as a scientific and educational public charity.
